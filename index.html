Chemitrails is being built in London, by a number of synthetic and biochemists. It aims to make the logging and sharing of chemicals studpidly easy. Each chemical deposited in the Chemtrails database is associated with standard NMR and Mass Spec data, for you to check against when you try to make the chemical your self. Additionally each entry on Chemtrails is associated with a DOI so this entry is fully citable in scientific journals and earns you credit for your grant applications. Chemtrails will hook into popular databases like Chem Spider and Figshare to provide a data rich log of every chemical you make in the lab.

Chemists work extremely hard in their labs designing and synthesising new molecules. Usually these are documented in the scientists lab book, but over time information that was not well organised or poorly described soon become impossible to repeat. Additionally NMR and mass spectrometry workups of the chemicals are sometimes lost in the jungle of the lab book. With Chemtrails following the synthesis and characterization of a molecule an entry is created on the website which fully documents the structure and synthesis method for the molecule. Furthermore NMR and mass spectrometry spectra can be attached to the entry as confirmation of the structure synthesised. All new submissions to Chemtrails are automatically assigned a DOI which means that each entry which includes the chemical structure, characterization and synthesis protocols can be easily cited in the scientific literature.
